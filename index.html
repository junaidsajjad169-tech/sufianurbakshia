Sufia Nurbakhshia: A Path of Unity

The Sufia Nurbakhshia is a distinct Islamic school of thought and a staunch advocate for inter-Islamic unity. Its foundation rests upon faith in Allah, His Angels, the Holy Quran and other divine scriptures, the Prophets and Messengers, and the Day of Judgment — as per the Quran, without any addition or omission.

Practically, it is based on the pillars of Islam: the Declaration of Faith (Kalima), the five daily prayers, fasting during Ramadan, Zakat, and the Pilgrimage (Hajj) to the House of Allah.

Regarding secondary matters (Furu-e-Deen), the school follows the book Fiqh al-Ahwat by Hazrat Shah Syed Muhammad Nurbakhsh (RA). In matters of fundamental principles (Usool), it adheres to his work Kitab al-Itiqadiyyah (Usool-e-I'tiqadiya), and for spiritual practices and litanies, it follows Da'wat-e-Sufia, attributed to Shah-e-Hamdan Mir Syed Ali Hamdani (RA).

As followers of Sufism, we hold Hazrat Mir Syed Muhammad Nurbakhsh (A.R.) in the same high regard as other schools hold Imam Abu Hanifa, Imam Malik, or Imam Shafi'i.

✦ ✦ ✦
A Bridge Between Traditions
What distinguishes this path is that it represents a middle way between the Sunni and Shia traditions — a standard-bearer for Muslim unity, offering a framework through which we can become one Ummah.
🤲

Prayer

According to Fiqh al-Ahwat, prayers may be performed with open arms (Sabal) or by folding them (Takattuf), accommodating both traditions.

💧

Ablution (Wudu)

One may perform Masah (wiping) over the feet or wash them if necessary — a flexibility that bridges different schools of practice.

👑

Leadership

We acknowledge both the Twelve Imams and the Rashidun Caliphs, honouring the full breadth of Islamic leadership.

🌿

Succession

For spiritual caliphate (Khilafat-e-Batini), we accept Hazrat Ali (KAW) as the first Imam; for temporal caliphate (Khilafat-e-Zahiri), Hazrat Abu Bakr Siddiq (RA) as the first Caliph.

🕌

Spiritual Retreat (I'tikaf)

Practiced in our mosques and Khanqahs even outside Ramadan, with strict discipline — no bedding is brought in and unnecessary contact with the outside world is avoided.

🌙

Commemoration of Muharram

Gatherings (Majalis) are held in mosques as worship, focusing on the philosophy of Imam Hussain's (AS) martyrdom. Self-flagellation, street processions, or chains are not part of our path.

✦ ✦ ✦
The Essence of the Path
Rooted in Islamic Sufism (Tasawwuf)

Our traditions are rooted in Islamic Sufism (Tasawwuf). The objective is spiritual development through a disciplined system of Murshid and Murid (Master and Disciple). The current Murshid is Mawlana Rustam Ali Anjum. This training aims for spiritual elevation, seeking proximity to God, and serving others.

These unique characteristics distinguish the Sufia Nurbakhshia as a prominent and refined path — a living bridge of unity within the greater family of Islam.

Chain of Transmission
The blessed silsila — the unbroken spiritual chain from the Prophet ﷺ to the present day

The Seal of Prophethood
Sayyiduna Muhammad al-Mustafa ﷺ
The source of all spiritual knowledge and the axis of the silsila. All Sufi chains trace back to the Prophet ﷺ through Imam Ali (AS).
Gate of Knowledge
Imam Ali ibn Abi Talib (AS)
The Commander of the Faithful and the first Imam — the direct inheritor of the esoteric sciences from the Prophet ﷺ.
Kubrawi Foundation
Shaykh Najm al-Din Kubra (1145–1221)
Founder of the Kubrawiyya order and spiritual progenitor of the Nurbakhshia lineage. Known as "the sculptor of souls."
The Guiding Pole
Shah Ni'matullah Wali (1330–1431)
The great master who bestowed the title "Nurbakhsh" upon his foremost disciple, recognizing his station as the light-giver of the age.
Founder of the Order
Syed Muhammad Nurbakhsh (1392–1464)
Author of the Risala-e-Sufiyya and numerous spiritual works. He established the Nurbakhshia order and codified its teachings on love, gnosis, and the path to Allah.
Consolidator of the Tradition
Syed Qasim Fayzabakhsh (d. 1515)
Son and successor of Nurbakhsh, who preserved and spread the order's teachings across Persia and the broader Islamic world.
Propagator in Baltistan
Mir Shamsuddin Iraqi (d. 1526)
The great missionary who brought the Nurbakhshia tradition to Baltistan, Kashmir, and the Subcontinent, transforming the spiritual landscape of the region.
The Living Masters
Contemporary Silsila — Present Day
The chain continues unbroken to this day. The living masters of the Nurbakhshia tradition guide seekers on the path, preserving the esoteric and exoteric sciences of Islam.
